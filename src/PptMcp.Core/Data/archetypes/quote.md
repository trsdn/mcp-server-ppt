# 12. Quote Slide

**When:** Adding a human voice, customer testimony, or expert opinion.

**Layout:**
```
Title: top (optional, or use slide as section break)
Background: subtle accent color or image with overlay
```

**CRITICAL: Quote slides must dramatize the outcome, not just display text.**

Structure the quote slide in two zones:
```
TOP ZONE (hero outcome):
  Bold lockup: "60% faster hiring" — 36pt bold, accent color, centered
  This is the visual anchor that makes the quote meaningful

BOTTOM ZONE (the quote):
  Quote text: 20-22pt, regular (NOT italic — italic reads as weak/uncertain)
  Large decorative open-quote mark: 80pt serif, Primary at 20% opacity
  Attribution: name, title, company — 14pt bold name + 12pt grey role
  Arranged as: Name (bold) | Title, Company (grey)
```

Rules:
- Never italic for the main quote — use regular weight for authority
- Pull out the key metric/outcome as a separate bold visual above the quote
- Attribution must include company size or context for credibility
- The slide should feel editorial/premium, not like a grey placeholder card

**QUANTIFIED TESTIMONIAL VARIANT:**

When the prompt includes **hard metrics + timeframe** (ROI %, payback period, growth rate, cost savings), FORCE the metric into the title or hero callout — never bury it in the quote:

**BEFORE-AFTER NPS PATTERN (CRITICAL for satisfaction metrics):**
When prompts include NPS changes with before/after values (e.g., "improved from 36 to 72"), make the metric progression the HERO EVIDENCE, not the quote:

```
HERO LOCKUP (large, centered):
  "NPS: 36 → 72" (48pt bold, accent color)
  "+36 point improvement" (24pt regular, grey, below main metric)

QUOTE ZONE (supporting evidence below):
  Quote text: 20pt regular, positioned at y=260pt
  Attribution: standard format as above
```

**Layout requirements for NPS before-after:**
- Position metric lockup at y=140pt (hero zone) 
- Use arrow (→) between before/after values for clear progression
- Include delta callout (+36 points) for immediate impact recognition
- Quote becomes secondary supporting evidence, NOT the primary focus
- Title should emphasize business impact: "Platform drives customer loyalty transformation"

**TITLE REQUIREMENTS for quantified quotes (MANDATORY):**
- **NEVER use metric labels**: "ROI Results", "Cost Savings", "Performance Metrics"
- **ALWAYS use implication-based conclusions**: State what the metric means and its business impact
- **Include the specific number**: The title must contain the exact metric value
- **Add context or next steps**: Answer "so what?" in the title itself

```
STRONG TITLE EXAMPLES:
❌ Weak: "ROI Results from Platform Implementation"  
✅ Strong: "300% ROI in 8 months validates platform as growth driver — scaling to all divisions"

❌ Weak: "Customer Efficiency Improvements"
✅ Strong: "60% faster hiring cycle enables 40% headcount growth target"

❌ Weak: "Cost Reduction Success"  
✅ Strong: "$2.4M annual savings exceed projections — reinvesting 50% in R&D expansion"
```

**HERO LOCKUP (required structure):**
  "[METRIC] in [TIMEFRAME]" — 42pt bold, centered at y=120pt
  Example: "300% ROI in 8 months" or "$2.4M saved annually"
  
**SUPPORTING QUOTE (evidence):**
  Quote validates WHY/HOW the metric was achieved
  Still 20-22pt regular, positioned at y=280pt

**ATTRIBUTION STANDARDS (mandatory for credibility):**
```
Full attribution: [Name] | [Title], [Company] ([Context]) — [Date/Timeframe]
Example: "Sarah Chen | VP Operations, TechCorp (500+ employees) — Q2 2024"
         "Mike Torres | CFO, GrowthCo (Series B, 2022 implementation) — March 2024"

REQUIRED elements:
- Full name (14pt bold)
- Exact title (12pt grey) 
- Company name (12pt grey)
- Context/scale (parentheses): company size, timeline, or implementation scope
- **DATE/TIMEFRAME (mandatory)**: When the quote/metric was captured (month/quarter/year)
  Format: "— Q2 2024" or "— March 2024" or "— 12 months post-launch"
```

**EVIDENCE STRUCTURE for ROI/savings/growth claims:**

**MANDATORY ATTRIBUTION for quantified metrics:**
- Context date: "Following Q3 2023 implementation..."
- Baseline reference: "Compared to previous manual process..."  
- Measurement period: "Data from 8-month pilot program..."
- Verification method: "Validated by third-party audit..."
- **Source timing: Always include when the metric was measured (month/quarter/year)**

**CO-EQUAL PROOF PATTERN for percentage improvements:**
When showing percentage gains, ALWAYS display both formats as equal visual weight:
```
PRIMARY: "60% faster hiring cycle"    (summary impact - 42pt bold)
PROOF:   "45 days → 18 days"         (before/after reality - 36pt regular)
```

**REQUIRED dual-format examples:**
- "300% ROI" MUST be paired with "$500K investment → $1.5M return"
- "40% cost reduction" MUST be paired with "$2.4M → $1.4M annual spend"  
- "60% faster processing" MUST be paired with "5 hours → 2 hours per case"
- "150% productivity gain" MUST be paired with "20 units/day → 50 units/day"

Position both metrics at same visual hierarchy — summary percentage and concrete numbers deserve equal prominence for credibility.

**SAAS/CUSTOMER-RETENTION QUOTE PATTERN (CRITICAL for subscription business prompts):**

When prompts mention **SaaS metrics, retention rates, churn reduction, or subscription growth**, ALWAYS use the proof-first pattern with quantified outcomes as the hero element:

**MANDATORY STRUCTURE for SaaS/retention quotes:**
```
HERO METRIC (top zone, 42pt bold, accent color):
  Primary KPI: "94% retention rate" or "Churn reduced to 2%" or "40% ARR growth"
  Context metric (24pt regular, grey): "Up from 78%" or "Down from 8%" or "vs. 15% industry avg"

EXPLANATORY QUOTE (middle zone, 20pt regular):
  Customer voice explaining HOW/WHY the metric was achieved
  Focus on workflow impact: "The platform eliminated the friction that was causing cancellations"
  
FULL ATTRIBUTION (bottom zone, standard format):
  Name | Title, Company (SaaS context) — Date
  Example: "Lisa Park | Head of Customer Success, DataFlow (B2B SaaS, 2K+ customers) — Q4 2023"
```

**REQUIRED ELEMENTS for SaaS/retention quotes:**
- **Top-line metric first**: Never bury retention rates, churn data, or growth metrics in the quote text
- **Comparative context**: Show improvement (before/after) or benchmark (vs. industry/competitors)
- **Workflow explanation**: Quote should explain the operational change that drove the metric
- **SaaS-specific attribution**: Include customer count, market segment, or subscription model context
- **Timeframe validation**: When the metric was measured (quarter/year) for recency credibility

**SaaS QUOTE CONTENT patterns:**
- Retention focus: "The intuitive dashboard reduced our support tickets by 60% — customers can self-serve now"
- Churn reduction: "We identified at-risk accounts 3 weeks earlier and saved 40% of them with proactive outreach" 
- Growth enablement: "Onboarding time dropped from 2 weeks to 3 days, so we can scale faster"
- Value realization: "Our customers see ROI in month 1 instead of month 6 — that's why they renew"

**SECURITY/COMPLIANCE QUOTE VARIANT:**

For security, compliance, or risk mitigation quotes, emphasize **trust indicators** and **risk reduction metrics**:

**HERO LOCKUP for security themes:**
- Risk reduction: "99.9% uptime maintained" or "Zero breaches in 24 months"
- Compliance achievement: "SOC 2 Type II certified" or "GDPR-compliant in 90 days"
- Trust metrics: "100% audit pass rate" or "Enterprise-grade security validated"

**SECURITY-SPECIFIC ATTRIBUTION context:**
- Include compliance credentials: "(CISO, Fortune 500 financial services)"
- Add regulatory context: "(Post-SOX implementation, 2023)"
- Specify industry risk level: "(Healthcare PHI, 50M+ records)"

**SECURITY QUOTE content emphasis:**
- Focus on peace of mind, not just efficiency: "Sleep better knowing our data is protected"
- Include specific security frameworks: "The platform met every SOC 2 requirement"
- Reference audit outcomes: "External penetration testing found zero vulnerabilities"

**OPERATIONAL IMPROVEMENT WITHOUT HARD BASELINES (CRITICAL for integration/synergy claims):**

When prompts claim operational improvements, synergy benefits, or process acceleration but **lack hard numeric baselines**, NEVER use decorative quote layouts. These claims require EVIDENCE STRUCTURE with proof context:

**MANDATORY STRUCTURE for non-quantified operational claims:**
```
TITLE ZONE (proof statement — 28pt bold):
  "Platform accelerated deal closure from discovery through approval" 
  NOT: "Improved Operational Efficiency" or "Integration Success"

EVIDENCE CONTEXT (supporting proof — 18pt regular, grey):
  From: "Manual 6-step approval process requiring C-suite sign-off"
  To: "Automated workflow with delegation rules and 48-hour SLA"
  Timeline: "Pilot completed Q3 2024, scaled Q4 2024"

QUOTE ZONE (operational details):
  Quote explains the workflow change and business impact
  Attribution includes implementation timeline and scope
```

**REQUIRED ELEMENTS for operational improvement quotes:**
- **Proof structure at top**: Title must state the acceleration/improvement with specificity
- **From/To context**: Define the baseline state vs. new state (process, timing, approach)  
- **Timeline evidence**: When the change was implemented and measured ("against previous quarter," "vs. legacy system")
- **Scope validation**: What was measured (pilot scope, department, customer segment)
- **Workflow explanation**: Quote should detail HOW the operational change works, not just that it works

**FORBIDDEN for operational claims:**
- ❌ Decorative quote-only layouts without proof context
- ❌ Vague improvement statements: "streamlined," "optimized," "enhanced"  
- ❌ Missing baseline reference: must compare against something specific
- ❌ Generic attribution without implementation timeline

**OPERATIONAL QUOTE CONTENT patterns:**
- Process change: "We went from 14-day manual approvals to same-day decisions with the new workflow"
- Integration benefit: "The unified dashboard eliminated 3 hours of daily reporting — our team focuses on strategy now"
- Synergy evidence: "Cross-platform data sync reduced duplicate entry from 40% of customer records to zero"

**TRADE-OFF RESOLVED CLAIMS (CRITICAL for "without hurting" or "plus" claims):**

When prompts include trade-off resolved language ("sustainability without hurting growth", "cost savings plus quality", "speed while maintaining accuracy"), BOTH sides of the claim must be represented in the HERO EVIDENCE zone — never leave one side implicit in the quote:

**MANDATORY PAIRED PROOF STRUCTURE:**
```
HERO EVIDENCE (top zone, side-by-side metrics):
  Left metric (36pt bold): "40% emissions reduction" 
  Right metric (36pt bold): "18% revenue growth"
  Context bar (18pt grey): "Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023 baseline"
  
QUOTE ZONE (supporting explanation):
  Quote explains HOW both outcomes were achieved simultaneously
  Attribution includes timeframe validation for both metrics
```

**REQUIRED ELEMENTS for trade-off resolved quotes:**
- **Both metrics explicit**: Never assume one side — show sustainability AND growth, cost savings AND quality  
- **Same timeframe**: Both metrics must use identical measurement periods for credibility
- **Causal explanation**: Quote must explain the operational approach that delivered both outcomes
- **Title includes both**: "Platform delivers 40% emissions cut while driving 18% revenue growth"
- **Visual pairing**: Position metrics side-by-side with equal visual weight (not hierarchy)

**FORBIDDEN for trade-off claims:**
- ❌ Single metric with implicit second benefit: showing only emissions while mentioning "without growth impact"
- ❌ Unequal evidence: detailed proof for one side, vague claims for the other
- ❌ Different timeframes: sustainability metric from Q4, growth data from full year
- ❌ Process-only explanation: explaining how without quantifying both outcomes
