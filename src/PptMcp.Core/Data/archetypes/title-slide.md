# Title Slide Patterns (Investor Presentations)

**When:** Opening slide for investor presentations, board meetings, or strategic executive briefings.

**CRITICAL: Investor Title Slides Must Be Substantive**

Unlike standard presentation title slides that only show topic and date, **investor presentation title slides require PROOF-FORWARD content** that signals strategic ambition and credibility:

**Mandatory elements for investor titles:**
- **Growth target with timeframe**: "$500M ARR by 2027" or "Path to $1B revenue"
- **Strategic driver**: "AI-led expansion" or "Platform consolidation strategy" 
- **Proof line**: "Already 40% to target with 3 major pilots confirmed"
- **Subtitle with urgency**: "Funding acceleration needed to capture 18-month market window"

**Layout for Investor Title Slides:**
```
Main Title (28pt bold): Strategic objective + target
  "Path to $500M ARR — AI-led expansion strategy"
  Position: x=480pt, y=180pt (centered)

Proof Line (18pt medium): Current progress + validation
  "Already 40% to target with 3 major enterprise pilots confirmed"
  Position: x=480pt, y=220pt (centered, 40pt below main)

Strategic Context (16pt regular, grey): 
  "Funding acceleration needed to capture 18-month market opportunity"
  Position: x=480pt, y=260pt (centered, 40pt below proof)

Footer: Company + Date + Confidentiality
  Position: x=36pt, y=500pt (standard footer zone)
```

**AVOID on investor titles:**
- Generic topic names without metrics ("Company Strategy" or "Growth Plan")
- Vague aspirations without proof ("Market Leadership Vision") 
- Missing timeframes ("Significant Growth Expected")
- No current progress indicators

## Cost Program Title Slide Pattern

**When:** Presenting cost reduction initiatives, operational efficiency programs, or savings-focused business cases.

**CRITICAL: Preserve Exact Metrics from Prompts**

When the prompt specifies a savings target (e.g., "$50M run-rate savings"), the title slide MUST preserve that exact number and metric rather than translating it into different terms.

**Required cost program title elements:**
- **Exact savings target**: Use the PRECISE number and timeframe from the prompt ("$50M run-rate savings", not "$50M annual savings")
- **Primary levers**: 2-3 specific cost reduction methods ("vendor consolidation + workforce optimization")
- **Audience context**: Why this matters now ("FY26 margin expansion" or "pre-IPO cost structure")
- **Progress indicator**: Current achievement vs. target ("$18M identified, $32M in pipeline")

**Layout for Cost Program Title Slides:**
```
Main Title (28pt bold): Savings Target + Primary Approach
  "$50M Run-Rate Savings Through Vendor Consolidation & Workforce Optimization"
  Position: x=480pt, y=180pt (centered)

Progress Line (18pt medium): Current state + validation
  "$18M savings identified, $32M in active pipeline with Q1 execution"
  Position: x=480pt, y=220pt (centered, 40pt below main)

Strategic Context (16pt regular, grey): 
  "Supporting FY26 margin expansion to 22% ahead of IPO readiness"
  Position: x=480pt, y=260pt (centered, 40pt below progress)
```

**Cost Program Anti-Patterns:**
- Translating "$50M run-rate" into "$50M annual" (changes the metric definition)
- Generic "cost reduction" without specific levers
- Missing progress indicators or current state
- Vague timeframes ("significant savings expected")

## Climate/ESG Title Slide Pattern

**When:** Presenting sustainability initiatives, climate action plans, ESG commitments, or emissions reduction programs.

**CRITICAL: Climate Title Slides Must Include Specific Targets**

Climate and ESG title slides must NOT default to generic governance language. Instead, they require **TARGET-FORWARD content** that states the specific commitment and timeframe in the title or subtitle:

**Mandatory elements for climate/ESG titles:**
- **Exact target with deadline**: "40% emissions reduction by 2030" or "Net-zero by 2035"
- **Strategic pathway**: Brief method reference ("via renewable energy + process optimization")
- **Baseline context line**: Optional short reference to starting point ("from 2020 baseline") or strategic implication ("supporting 1.5°C pathway")
- **Avoid committee language**: Never use generic phrases like "climate governance review" or "ESG framework update"

**Layout for Climate/ESG Title Slides:**
```
Main Title (28pt bold): Specific Target + Deadline
  "40% Emissions Reduction by 2030"
  Position: x=480pt, y=180pt (centered)

Strategic Context (18pt medium): Pathway + baseline reference
  "Via renewable energy transition and process optimization from 2020 baseline"
  Position: x=480pt, y=220pt (centered, 40pt below main)

Strategic Implication (16pt regular, grey): 
  "Supporting 1.5°C global warming pathway and sustainable growth strategy"
  Position: x=480pt, y=260pt (centered, 40pt below context)

Footer: Company + Date + Confidentiality
  Position: x=36pt, y=500pt (standard footer zone)
```

**Climate/ESG Anti-Patterns:**
- Generic governance titles: "Climate Action Update" or "ESG Framework Review"
- Missing specific targets or deadlines
- Committee-style language instead of commitment statements
- No baseline or pathway reference
- Vague sustainability claims without quantified targets

## Product Launch Title Slide Pattern

**When:** Announcing new products, platform launches, feature releases, or go-to-market initiatives.

**CRITICAL: Launch Title Slides Must Preserve Event Framing**

Product launch title slides must NOT default to generic product positioning or corporate slogans. Instead, they require **LAUNCH-SPECIFIC content** that frames this as an event announcement with clear value proposition:

**Mandatory elements for product launch titles:**
- **Launch headline**: Event-driven language ("Introducing", "Launching", "Now Available") + product name
- **Product/platform name**: Clear, specific product identifier (not category or company name)
- **Value proposition line**: One concise line stating target audience benefit or key capability
- **Event framing preservation**: Must maintain the announcement context, not just product description

**Layout for Product Launch Title Slides:**
```
Main Title (28pt bold): Launch headline + Product name
  "Introducing SalesMax Pro — Advanced Pipeline Intelligence Platform"
  Position: x=480pt, y=180pt (centered)

Value Line (18pt medium): Target audience + key benefit
  "Real-time deal scoring and automated forecasting for enterprise sales teams"
  Position: x=480pt, y=220pt (centered, 40pt below main)

Launch Context (16pt regular, grey): 
  "Available March 2026 — Early access for existing SalesMax customers"
  Position: x=480pt, y=260pt (centered, 40pt below value)
```

**Product Launch Anti-Patterns:**
- Generic slogans without launch framing ("Transforming Sales Forever")
- Product categories instead of specific names ("Our New CRM Platform")
- Missing target audience or value clarity ("Revolutionary Technology")
- Corporate positioning instead of launch event ("Company Innovation Strategy")
- Broad positioning messages instead of specific launch value ("Leading the Future")

## Integration Review Title Slide Pattern

**When:** Post-merger integration reviews, PMI status updates, synergy tracking presentations, or milestone assessment meetings.

**CRITICAL: Distinguish Committee Updates from Action Titles**

Integration review title slides must NOT default to generic committee language. Instead, they require **PROGRESS-CONCLUSION content** that states specific progress status and next actions, not just meeting type:

**Mandatory elements for integration title slides:**
- **Progress conclusion with status**: "Synergy capture 65% complete" or "Integration milestone 2 of 4 achieved"
- **Specific achievement or escalation**: "Cost synergies delivered $18M, revenue synergies need Q2 acceleration" 
- **Avoid committee language**: Never use generic phrases like "Integration Review Committee Update" or "PMI Status Meeting"
- **One lightweight proof line**: For status-heavy updates, include current progress indicator to signal achievement level

**Layout for Integration Review Title Slides:**
```
Main Title (28pt bold): Progress Status + Key Conclusion
  "Integration 65% Complete — Revenue Synergies Require Q2 Acceleration"

Subtitle (18pt regular, Primary color): Progress Proof Line
  "$18M cost synergies delivered; 3 of 5 system integrations operational"

Context Line (14pt, Text Medium): Meeting/Timeline Context
  "PMI Steering Committee — March 2026 Milestone Review"
```

**Examples:**

| Weak (committee-focused) | Strong (progress-action focused) |
|---|---|
| "Q1 Integration Review Committee Update" | "Integration 40% complete — IT systems merger behind schedule" |
| "PMI Status Meeting" | "Synergy target 70% achieved — procurement consolidation accelerating" |
| "Post-Merger Integration Assessment" | "Cultural integration on track, cost synergies need leadership action" |

**When to use progress conclusions:**
- **Synergy capture status**: "65% of targeted synergies captured" with specific achievement numbers
- **Milestone attainment**: "Phase 2 integration completed 2 weeks early" with concrete deliverables
- **Issue escalation**: "Revenue integration stalled — requires executive intervention" with clear problem statement
